About

Reporting from
the field.

I'm Emmett Gartner, a climate and environment reporter with a decade of experience covering the intersection of ecology, policy, and human lives. My work takes me from flooded coastal towns to drought-stricken farmlands, from UN climate summits to community hearings.

Before going independent, I was a staff reporter at The Atlantic and a fellow at the Columbia School of Journalism. My writing has appeared in The New Yorker, The Guardian, National Geographic, and ProPublica.

I believe in rigorous, on-the-ground reporting. Climate change is the defining story of our era — I'm committed to covering it with the depth and nuance it demands.
